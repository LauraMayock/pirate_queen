On the trail of Ireland's legendary pirate queeen

  • 1530
    Born
    In the lorship of Umhall, Co Mayo, daughter of the chieftain Owen (Dudara) O’Malley from whom she learned her seafaring expertise.
  • 1546
    Married
    Given in a politically motivated marriage to Donal of the Battles O’Flaherty, tanaiste of the Clan O’Flaherty in his castle of Bunowen, in Connemara.
  • 1547-1552
    Mother of two
    Gives birth to 2 sons, Owen and Murrough and a daughter, Margaret.
  • 1560
    Title 4
    Donal killed in an inter-clan dispute. Grace avenges his death and assumes leadership of his clan on behalf of her sons.
  • 1564
    Died
    Returns to Umhall and settles on Clare Island, from where she commences her successful career of ’maintenance by land and sea’ with her father’s ships and a private army of 200 men. Her fame as a leader and an expert mariner grows.
  • 1565
    Born
    Rescues Hugh de Lacy from the sea and he becomes her lover. Subsequently wreaks a terrible retribution on the MacMahons when they kill Hugh.
  • 1546
    Married
    Given in a politically motivated marriage to Donal of the Battles O’Flaherty, tanaiste of the Clan O’Flaherty in his castle of Bunowen, in Connemara.
  • 1566
    Mother of two
    When the English administration begins to push into Mayo, she marries Richard-in-Iron Bourke, whose castle, Rockfleet, is less exposed than Clare Island. When she has moved her ships and army into Richard’s castle she divorces him.
  • 1560
    Title 4
    Donal killed in an inter-clan dispute. Grace avenges his death and assumes leadership of his clan on behalf of her sons.
  • 1567
    Died
    Her son Theobald (Tiboid-ne-Long) Toby-of-the- Ships is born aboard her ship. She defends her new-born son from an attack from Barbary Pirates On her return to Rockfleet she becomes re-united with Richard-in-Iron to protect themselves from the English.
  • 1571
    Born
    With Grace’s help, Richard-in-Iron becomes tanaiste (elected succesor) to the MacWilliam of Mayo, the premier chiefdom in Mayo
  • 1576
    Married
    The Mac William of Mayo submits to Queen Elizabeth of England. Richard-in-Iron’s position as his successor is under threat.
  • 1577
    Mother of two
    With her army and navy in tow, Grace impresses Elizabeth’s minister, Sir Henry Sidney in Galway with her military capabilities.
  • 1577
    Title 4
    Grace plunders Desmond and is captured by the Earl of Desmond, who imprisons her in Limerick Jail.
